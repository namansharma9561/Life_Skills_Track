# Question 2
### How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P?

B = MAP stands for:

- **B** = Behavior  
- **M** = Motivation  
- **A** = Ability  
- **P** = Prompt  

A habit becomes easier when:
- we feel motivated,
- the task is simple,
- and there is a reminder to do it.

For example, keeping a water bottle near the desk can remind us to drink more water.

# Question 3
### Why is it important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit?

Celebrating after completing a habit gives positive feelings to the brain. This makes the brain remember the activity as rewarding and increases the chances of repeating the habit again.

# Question 6
### Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

The book suggests making habits simple and convenient. We can:
- start with very small actions,
- prepare the environment,
- reduce distractions,
- and make the habit easy to begin.

For example, placing workout clothes nearby makes exercising easier.

---

# Question 7
### Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

To reduce bad habits, the book recommends increasing difficulty and removing triggers. We can:
- hide distractions,
- make access difficult,
- or add extra steps before doing the habit.

For example, uninstalling social media apps can reduce unnecessary usage.
